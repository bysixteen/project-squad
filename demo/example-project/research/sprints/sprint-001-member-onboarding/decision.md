## Decision

We will build the invite-link onboarding flow as designed in this sprint. The flow is: invite generated by secretary → member taps link → three-step onboarding (name, position, confirm) → member lands on squad list with their name highlighted. The User/Member entity separation proposed by Marcus is adopted as a core data model decision.

## Rationale

The invite-link model was chosen in Sprint 000 and the team remains confident in it. The GDPR risk of open registration — collecting data from people who may not be genuine club members — is a real constraint, not a hypothetical one. The club-code alternative proposed by Elias reduces secretary burden but does not eliminate the GDPR concern.

The one-question-per-screen pattern was the clear winner in the sketch phase. It is technically straightforward, works well on mobile, and creates the conversational feel that Leo identified as important for first impressions.

The User/Member entity separation is a slightly higher upfront cost but avoids a painful migration later when multi-club support becomes a requirement. It is the right call.

## Elias Vance's Dissent

Elias challenged the invite-link model on the grounds that it creates a dependency on secretary proactivity. His proposed alternative — open registration with a club code — would reduce per-member secretary burden. The team acknowledged the concern but maintained the invite-link decision on GDPR grounds. **Elias's dissent is recorded in `research/dissent-register.md` with a review trigger: revisit if member adoption falls below 60% of invited members completing registration.**

## What We Are NOT Doing

- **Not building a full user profile in this sprint.** Name, email, and playing position only. No photos, bio, or social links.
- **Not building the secretary invite management UI in this sprint.** Invite generation will be a hardcoded server action for now. The full admin UI is a separate sprint.
- **Not using client-side state for onboarding progress.** Progress is stored in the database against the invite token to handle mid-flow abandonment.

## Next Action

Begin implementation of the invite-link flow. Kira leads on the database schema and server actions; Lena leads on the onboarding UI components including the SVG pitch diagram. Marcus to confirm the User/Member data model before any schema migrations are written.
