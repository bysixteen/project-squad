Four rules for running sprints at scale

RULE_01 FOCUS
One moment per sprint
If you find yourself writing two sprint questions about different moments, split it into two sprints. Focused sprints produce clear decisions. Broad sprints produce noise.
RULE_02 EFFICIENCY
The Brain compounds
Each sprint makes the next one cheaper. By Sprint 005, the AI has deep context from PRINCIPLES.md and DECISIONS.md and barely needs re-explaining.
RULE_03 VELOCITY
Spikes run in parallel
A spike only blocks a sprint if the sprint depends on its answer. Otherwise, run them concurrently. Treat technical investigations as separate workstreams.
RULE_04 SAFETY
Dissent review triggers fire
Overruled concerns from early sprints aren't lost. They surface automatically when later sprints hit the relevant territory or trigger condition. Nothing is forgotten.
